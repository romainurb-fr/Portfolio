Introduction

This project aims to study the vibrational behavior of a helicopter blade affected by a mass imbalance in the tail rotor. To do so, we developed several simplified models of a helicopter incorporating the defect, and analyzed their dynamic response during rotation in order to understand the impact of vibrations on the tail and cabin structure.

Methodology

In this study, we sought to accurately model the vibrational phenomena of a helicopter using two models, each divided into two sub-models, ranging from the simplest to the most realistic. The first model treats the cabin as a rigid body, with the tail represented as a spring and the blades as a point mass; a damper is later added to refine the system‚Äôs behavior. The second model considers the cabin as an independent mass connected to another mass via a spring, and then further improved with a damper, to better simulate real-world dynamics.

Results

The results showed that the mass defect had a significant influence on the system‚Äôs vibration modes. Resonant frequencies were identified, which could present safety risks if left unaddressed.

Conclusion

This study highlights the importance of accounting for mass imbalances in the vibrational analysis of helicopter blades. Recommendations include performing regular inspections and considering damping and structural corrections in future designs.
